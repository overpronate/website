1.1 Describing the purpose of IT in modern business

IT is vital to conducting business in the modern world; most all businesses today require an IT department to operate and compete in the market. You can break down the main areas used to internal and external, for instance companies will use IT for internal communications (email/video conferencing), file sharing (DropBox), collaborative working (Trello/Slack) etc. and they may also have a department dealing with reaching externally to customers through app development, engaging the public on social media platforms, and yet another protecting their and their customers’ assets with cyber security. Even aside from large corporations, almost all wages are process and paid through PAYE tax solutions, and most government services are accessed online, speeding up the processing of citizens’ applications, streamlining complex systems across departments, and allowing databases to be cross checked, reducing fraud etc. The ability to store large databases online has streamlined stock processing, tracking sales and therefore profits, and given birth to entire new sectors of industry and employment. Whilst jobs have been lost to automation in factories and data processing superseding scores of people manually processing paperwork, sectors have emerged in encryption and cyber security, 3D animation and design, and data processing can be reduced from days and weeks of pouring over raw data to plot trends, to the simple click of a button manipulating tables and producing infographics.

1.2 Describing methods, skills and resources needed to complete digital projects successfully – what do you need to make projects successful? What tools? What skills?

Aside from the obvious need to have stable and reliable access to computing equipment and internet, you need a way to plan and track the progress of your project, and if in a team communicating with your colleagues. Successfully working as a team can be made much easier with instantly collaborative programs such as Trello and Jira, open communication such as Stand Ups, and having a team structure massively aids its productivity, and prevents stepping on each others cyber toes. In a team, having a broad range of specialties and skills can be beneficial; there is no point in 10 back end developers and no front end, or 50 programmers and no team lead. Depending on the scope of the project, you may need team members with image manipulation/video editing skills, server management, or representatives from industry you are producing the project for. Having all of your work uploaded and accessible on the cloud means that all members can access it regardless of location and timezone, and have up to date copies; the common industry standard being GitHub, Google Documents, or DropBox.

1.4 Describe the risks that might impact digital projects – what causes digital projects to fail? Why?

For a project to run to completion, and delivered on time, you need sufficient funding, staffing, and premises. Withdrawal of funds/market crashes, staff shortages, or eviction from rented offices would derail most projects. Poor understanding, or dramatic change, of the target market could ruin a project’s success. Examples of this would include not understanding how the digital project will be used, misjudging market size, and misunderstanding who and what your competitors are. Finally poor communications and bad management. In order for a project to succeed it requires many parties to come together in agreement about how the project should progress. Miscommunication can lead to misunderstanding between two parties, whether it is between coworkers, client, or staff. Bad management can have the single biggest influence on any projects success, with staff being willing to weather most storms for a good manager.

1.5 Describe how you would go about selecting and using IT systems and software – how do you select the right kind of technology for the job?

While using the optimal tool for the job is ideal, familiarity is worth a significant amount when weighing up the pros and cons of one set of tools, and as such I will use Trello to plan out my project, Slack to communicate with colleagues, and GitHub to store my files oline. Stability, community, and support are all extremely valuable, especially when starting out. You never want to be the biggest fish using a tool if you can help it, shouldering yourself with the burdens on maintenance and new arising problems that haven’t been encountered by others. It is worth considering the longevity of both the project and the technology, balancing cutting edge/emerging technology as well as the budget you have to work with.

1.6 Describe and provide analysis on how your chosen technologies have helped you achieve your outcomes. How has trello helped you plan and how can git and GitHub help with your codes version control?

As a visual person, I have found Trello a natural digital progression of planning with sticky notes, with the added benefit of being able to access it anywhere, and work with input from other people. What you lose from the visceral satisfaction of crumpling up a completed task, you gain in being able to retrieve archived items, labels, and being able to rearrange tasks to your hearts content. For any long lived project maintaining a history of changes is essential. Git provides an interface for doing this that is much more powerful than creating “copy 1”, “copy 2”, etc. of a project. Additionally git and github provide tools for collaborating with others; for sharing code, and making contributions to a project in readable and reviewable chunks.

1.7 Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects – How does GDPR impact digital projects?

With the very essence of the internet being global, you need to be aware of major legislation in different countries to be able to provide an international product. The most widespread examples of these are COPPA, requiring age verification, CAN-SPAM, regulating commercial emails and the consumers rights to unsubscribe from their mailing list, and GDPR, regulating the way that personal data can be used and shared, and the timespan it can be stored for.
